# Quick Task 260428-b21 — Summary

## What changed

- **`src/lib/cohortAgg.ts`** — `cohortRepeaterCountByVisitBucket` now filters with `total_repeaters >= 1` instead of the shared `SPARSE_MIN_COHORT_SIZE` (= 5). Replaced the JSDoc paragraph above the function to flag the deviation. Dropped the now-unused `SPARSE_MIN_COHORT_SIZE` import.
- **`tests/unit/cohortAgg.test.ts`** — replaced the old "sparse-filters cohorts with fewer than 5 repeaters" test with one asserting the new behavior: a cohort with a single repeater is kept. The adjacent zero-repeaters test still covers the "all first-timers → empty result" case.

## Why

The repeater cohort chart's rightmost visible bar was the cohort starting **2026-04-06** even though `customer_ltv_v` had data through 2026-04-27. Cause: `SPARSE_MIN_COHORT_SIZE = 5` dropped any cohort whose customers hadn't yet made 5 repeat visits in aggregate. The Apr 13 / 20 / 27 cohorts were silently filtered as "sparse" — but they're not sparse, they're just **young**.

Lowering the filter to `>= 1` lets recent cohorts show up. Their naturally short stack heights signal "still maturing" without a separate badge. Cohorts with zero repeaters (e.g. Apr 27, only 1 day old) still produce zero-height stacks → invisible regardless.

Per user direction: "Q1: yes add it for sake of simplicity."
